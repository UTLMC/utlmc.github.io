    👋 Good morning, LMC!

    Welcome to the Light Music Club, a space for musicians of all backgrounds to create and play music from anime, video games, J-pop, J-rock, vocaloids, and more! We are a branch of the University of Toronto Student Anime & Manga Association (UT-SAMA) based in the St. George campus.

    Anisongs, OSTs, and More

    Rock bands, jazz groups, and chamber ensembles formed at LMC have performed everything from video game soundtracks and anime openings to indie V-pop and jazz standards.

    Catch us Live!

    LMC performs at anime conventions like Genshiken Festival, organizes annual concerts, and posts music on Youtube. We also hold jam sessions, workshops, karaokes, and more!

    About

    The Light Music Club (LMC) is the only university club in Toronto dedicated to performing music from anime, video games, and J-pop among other genres. It is a casual, student-led group comprised of volunteer musicians of all backgrounds and skill levels. The LMC prides itself on the diversity of its programmes, its openness to musicians whether experienced or new, and the hard work its members put in to bring songs to life.

    LMC operations, from rehearsals to concerts, revolve around university semesters. The club centrally organizes repertoire but also encourages members to form independent ensembles, giving the opportunity for everyone to play music they personally enjoy.

    History

    Music activity in UT-SAMA dates back from 2011 to 2017, with several groups having taken on the “Light Music Club” label at different points in time. The current iteration of LMC began in September 2023 under the leadership of Stone, Sean, and Alexis. As a humble group of 13, the founding band of LMC made its inaugural performance at the UT-SAMA 20th Anniversary event in April 2024. Since then, LMC has hit many milestones:

    • 2024-08: First music cover on LMC Youtube channel
    • 2024-10: First self-organized concert
    • 2025-08: This website goes live!
    • 2025-09: LMC's ranks reach 50 members

    Repertoire Archive

    The LMC repertoire features two types of projects. Large ensemble projects involve many players and varied instrument groups; owing to their scope and complexity, they are decided by popular vote before the term starts and centrally coordinated by the club. Small ensemble projects are self-initiated and lead by motivated LMC members who coordinate song-writing and rehearsals (or recordings) with club support.

    Location

    Large ensemble rehearsals take place at the Ontario Institute for Studies in Education (OISE) at 252 Bloor Street West, in room C154 underground.

    The building is directly connected to the subway via the Bedford Road exit at St. George station, which leads to floor C. There is also vehicle parking and bicycle parking. See the OISE website for details.

    We have historically performed concerts at the OISE Auditorium and the Cat's Eye in the Goldring Student Centre (150 Charles Street West) at Victoria College.

    Concerts

    The LMC typically holds live concerts twice a year near the end of the University of Toronto’s fall and winter semesters, where we showcase music from large ensembles, small ensembles, and recording projects. Live attendance costs money, but recordings of our previous concerts are freely available on our Youtube channel.
